# Kurodoko Rules

Kurodoko (also known as Kuromasu or "Where is Black Cells?") is a shading puzzle played on a rectangular grid with numbered cells.

The objective is to shade some cells black while leaving others white, guided by numbered clues.

## Rules

1. Some cells must be shaded black; others remain white.
2. Numbered cells cannot be shaded black.
3. Each number indicates how many white cells are visible from that cell, counting horizontally and vertically in all four directions until blocked by a black cell or the grid edge. The numbered cell itself is included in this count.
4. Other numbered cells are treated as white for counting purposes.
5. All white cells must be connected orthogonally in a single group.
6. Black cells cannot be orthogonally adjacent to each other (diagonal adjacency is allowed).

## Variations

* Kurodoko can be played on non-rectangular grids.
