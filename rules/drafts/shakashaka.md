# Shakashaka Rules

Shakashaka (also known as Proof of Quilt) is a puzzle played on a rectangular grid with some black cells containing numbers.

The objective is to place right-angled triangles in white cells to create rectangular or square white areas.

## Rules

1. The grid contains both black cells (which may contain numbers) and white cells.
2. White cells may either remain empty or contain a right-angled triangle occupying half the cell (divided diagonally).
3. Numbers in black cells indicate how many triangles are orthogonally adjacent (horizontally and vertically) to that cell.
4. All remaining white space (after placing triangles) must form rectangles or squares, which may be axis-aligned or rotated 45 degrees.
5. White rectangles and squares cannot share an edge with each other.

## Variations

* Shakashaka can be played on non-rectangular grids.
