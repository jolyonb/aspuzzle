# LITS Rules

LITS (formerly known as Nuruomino) is a shading puzzle played on a rectangular grid divided into regions, where each region contains at least four cells.

The objective is to shade exactly four cells in each region to form a tetromino according to the following rules.

## Rules

1. Exactly four cells must be shaded in each region, forming a single connected tetromino shape.
2. The tetrominoes must be one of the four "LITS" shapes: L, I, T, or S (including their rotations and reflections). The O (square) tetromino is forbidden by rule 5.
3. Two tetrominoes of the same type cannot share an edge, even if one is a rotation or reflection of the other.
4. All shaded cells must form a single orthogonally connected group.
5. No 2x2 area may be entirely shaded.

## Variations

* LITS can be played on non-rectangular grids.
* Some variations allow different sets of polyominoes.
