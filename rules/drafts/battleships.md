# Battleships Rules

Battleships (also known as Bimaru or Solitaire Battleships) is a puzzle played on a rectangular grid where ships must be located.

The objective is to find the positions of all hidden ships according to the following rules.

## Rules

1. A fleet of ships must be placed on the grid. The number and sizes of ships are specified for each puzzle.
2. Each ship occupies a straight horizontal or vertical line of consecutive cells.
3. Ships cannot touch each other, not even diagonally.
4. Numbers outside the grid indicate how many cells are occupied by ships in each row and column.
5. Some cells may be pre-revealed, showing water, ship segments, or ship endpoints.

## Variations

* The standard fleet typically consists of one 4-cell ship, two 3-cell ships, three 2-cell ships, and four 1-cell ships.
* Different puzzles may use different fleet configurations.
* Battleships can be played on non-square grids.
